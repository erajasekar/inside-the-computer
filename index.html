CPU ‚Äì The Brain of the Computer

The CPU (Central Processing Unit) is like the brain of your computer! Just like your brain controls everything you do, the CPU controls everything the computer does. It reads instructions and tells all the other parts what to do.

When you click on something, type a message, or play a game, the CPU is working super fast to make it happen. Modern CPUs can do billions of calculations every single second!

Did you know? Some CPUs are smaller than a coin but are more powerful than entire computers from 30 years ago! The fastest CPUs can execute over 100 billion instructions per second!

GPU ‚Äì The Graphics Powerhouse

The GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) is like an amazing artist that creates all the pictures and videos you see on your screen! While the CPU is great at thinking, the GPU is fantastic at drawing and making things look beautiful.

Every time you watch a video, play a game, or even move your mouse cursor, the GPU is working hard to make sure everything looks smooth and colorful. It's especially important for games and creative work!

A modern gaming GPU has thousands of tiny processors working together! While a CPU might have 8-16 cores, a GPU can have over 2,000 cores all working at the same time!

RAM ‚Äì The Computer's Short-Term Memory

RAM (Random Access Memory) is like your computer's short-term memory! Just like you remember what you had for breakfast this morning, RAM helps your computer remember what it's working on right now.

When you open a program or a game, it gets loaded into RAM so the computer can access it super quickly. The more RAM you have, the more things your computer can remember and work with at the same time!

RAM is super fast but forgets everything when you turn off your computer! That's why you need to save your work. Modern RAM can transfer data at speeds of over 25 GB per second!

Storage ‚Äì The Computer's Long-Term Memory

Storage is like your computer's long-term memory and filing cabinet all in one! This is where your computer keeps all your photos, videos, games, and programs safe, even when it's turned off.

There are two main types: Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) that spin like record players, and Solid State Drives (SSDs) that have no moving parts and are super fast! SSDs make your computer start up much quicker.

A single modern SSD can hold over 1 million photos or 250,000 songs! The first computer hard drive from 1956 was as big as two refrigerators but could only store 5MB of data!

Motherboard ‚Äì The Computer's Nervous System

The motherboard is like the nervous system of your computer! Just like your nervous system connects your brain to all parts of your body, the motherboard connects all the computer parts together so they can talk to each other.

It's a big green board with lots of tiny pathways called circuits. Every part of your computer plugs into the motherboard - the CPU, RAM, GPU, and storage all connect here to work as a team!

A motherboard can have thousands of tiny connections! The pathways are so small that you could fit hundreds of them in the width of a human hair. They're made with real gold because gold never rusts!

Power Supply ‚Äì The Computer's Heart

The Power Supply Unit (PSU) is like the heart of your computer! Just like your heart pumps blood to every part of your body, the PSU sends electricity to every part of your computer to keep it alive and working.

It takes the electricity from your wall outlet (which is too strong) and makes it just the right amount for each computer part. Different parts need different amounts of power, and the PSU makes sure everyone gets exactly what they need!

Your wall outlet provides 120V of electricity, but computer parts only need 3V, 5V, or 12V! The PSU is like a transformer that changes dangerous electricity into safe, usable power for your computer.
